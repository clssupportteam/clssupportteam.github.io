Offering General Guidance:

TUO

When you registered the death, did you use the “Tell us Once” service?

Note - Tailor advice around whether they have registered death yet or not.

"The Tell Us Once Service notifies all government departments (such as DWP, DVLA, Passport, Benefits, Council Tax, HMRC and local Council for Council Tax) We recommend you use this service if you haven’t done so already as it will save you making multiple phone calls."


Car insurance and Tax

Tell Us Once will deal with the government departments, one thing to consider is the DVLA will get notified is there any vehicles to consider?

Note - Any unused car tax will be refunded

If you are intending to drive the vehicle you will need to insure and tax the vehicle in your name You may also want to consider to SORN the vehicle until any decision is made on what to do with the vehicle.

Note - If selling the NOK/Exec can do this and send V5 paperwork to the DVLA Sensitive Case work team, with a copy of the death certificate to process change of ownership.


Home Insurance

May I ask, did your [Insert Relationship] own their property?

Note - It is important to continue to insure and secure the property now, especially if it is unoccupied. We recommend you make contact with the home insurer to discuss ongoing cover.


Council tax

Is anyone living in the property?

[ If Yes ] : If you (Or Someone else) are now living in the property alone, you may qualify for the sole occupancy discount. Tell Us Once does notify the local council, but it could take several weeks to affect the payments, so you can always contact the local authority direct either on-line or by phone to get payments reduced sooner.

[ If No ] : Its possible the property could be exempt from council tax for a short period, please notify the local authority either on-line or by phone to discuss with them.


Bereavement Support Payment

Note - This is only applicable if there is a surviving spouse/civil partner/partner

Option 1 – Spouse/civil partner/living with a partner who are under state pension age. They could be eligible for the Bereavement Support Payment. If there are children under the age of 18 they could qualify for the maximum amount

There is a possibility that [ you / the spouse/civil partner / the partner ] may qualify for the Bereavement Support Payment. So long as [you / the spouse/civil partner / the partner] are/is under state pension age, and depending on the deceased’s National Insurance contributions, you may be eligible for this payment. This payment is not means tested, and depending on circumstances can be as much as an initial lump sum of £3,500 plus a monthly payment over 18 months.

To ensure you receive the full amount due you MUST apply for it within 3 months of your [insert relationship] passing.If you have a pen handy, I can give you details of how to check and apply for this - You can apply online via Gov.UK or calling bereavement services helpline on 0800 151 2012, or by post (details on UKGOV.com)


Option 2 - Spouse/ Civil Partner /partner are over state pension age

You may want to speak to Department Works and Pensions about your own situation to see if they qualify for other payment/allowances now the deceased has passed.
